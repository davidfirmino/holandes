# Functional grammar, reading patterns, and traps — official A2 style

This is not an academic grammar file. It explains the structures that appear most often in official/real A2 reading simulations and how they are usually tested.

## 1. Small words that often contain the answer

### vanaf = from / starting from

Examples:

- `vanaf volgende maand` = from next month
- `vanaf vrijdag` = from Friday
- `vanaf 1 maart` = from March 1
- `vanaf 8 uur` = from 8 o’clock

Exam trap: it does not mean before. It marks the start of a new situation.

### voor / vóór = before

Examples:

- `vóór dinsdag` = before Tuesday
- `voor 15 april` = before April 15
- `voor 1 augustus` = before August 1
- `voor 9:30 uur bellen` = call before 9:30

Exam trap: in deadline questions, `voor` usually means you must act before that date or time.

### uiterlijk = no later than / at the latest

Examples:

- `uiterlijk een maand van tevoren` = no later than one month in advance
- `uiterlijk een uur voordat de les begint` = no later than one hour before the lesson starts
- `uiterlijk op het tijdstip dat uw werkdag begint` = no later than the time your workday starts

Exam trap: `uiterlijk` marks a hard deadline. If the lesson starts at 9:00 and the text says `uiterlijk een uur voordat de les begint`, the latest time is 8:00.

### tot = until

Examples:

- `tot 20.00 uur` = until 8 p.m.
- `tot 17.00 uur` = until 5 p.m.
- `tot 2 mei` = until May 2

### tot en met = up to and including

Examples:

- `tot en met donderdag` = up to and including Thursday
- `maandag tot en met donderdag` = Monday through Thursday, including Thursday

Exam trap: `tot en met` clearly includes the final day.

### tijdens = during

Examples:

- `tijdens openingstijden` = during opening hours
- `tijdens de toets` = during the test
- `tijdens de vakantie` = during the holiday

### daarna = after that

Examples:

- `Daarna geeft Nadia de lessen Nederlands` = after that, Nadia teaches the Dutch lessons.
- `Daarna gaat het hele gebouw weer open` = after that, the whole building opens again.

### daarvan / daarmee / daarover

| Dutch | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| daarvan | from that / with that money | `Daarvan hebben we een boek gekocht.` |
| daarmee | with that | `wat u daarmee kunt maken` |
| daarover | about that | `Daarover zoeken we samen informatie.` |

These words refer back to something mentioned earlier. In A2 reading, you need to identify what they refer to.

## 2. Obligation, permission, and option

### moeten = must / have to

Examples:

- `U moet zich inschrijven.` = You must register.
- `Je moet deze toets nog wel doen.` = You still have to take this test.
- `De rest moet extra dagen werken.` = The rest must work extra days.

### mogen = may / be allowed to

Examples:

- `Je mag geen woordenboek gebruiken.` = You may not use a dictionary.
- `Je mag maximaal twee weken vakantie opnemen.` = You may take a maximum of two weeks of holiday.
- `Klanten mogen hun oude klantenkaart niet meer gebruiken.` = Customers may no longer use their old customer card.

### hoeven niet = do not have to / not necessary

Examples:

- `Je hoeft niet meer te werken als je dat niet wilt.` = You do not have to work more if you do not want to.
- `Dat hoeven jullie mij niet te vragen.` = You do not have to ask me that.
- `U hoeft geen medische gegevens te geven.` = You do not have to give medical data.
- `Je hoeft geen zwemkleding mee te nemen.` = You do not have to bring swimwear.

Exam trap: `hoeven niet` is not a prohibition. It means something is not required.

### moeten wel = do have to / must indeed

Example:

- `U hoeft geen medische gegevens te geven. U moet wél vertellen waarom u niet kunt komen werken.`

Meaning: you do not have to share medical details, but you do have to explain generally why you cannot come to work.

### kunnen = can / be able to

Examples:

- `U kunt een folder downloaden.` = You can download a brochure.
- `U kunt zich aanmelden bij het sportcentrum.` = You can register at the sports center.
- `Als u Chantal niet kunt bereiken...` = If you cannot reach Chantal...

### willen = want

Examples:

- `mensen die meer willen werken` = people who want to work more
- `Wilt u zich inschrijven?` = Do you want to register?
- `Wil je mee met de bus?` = Do you want to go along by bus?

## 3. Cause and consequence

### omdat = because / since

After `omdat`, the verb often moves to the end of the clause.

Examples:

- `Omdat we langer open zijn, zoek ik mensen.`
- `Ik mail je, omdat wij maandag onze presentatie moeten geven.`

### daarom = therefore / for that reason

Examples:

- `Daarom wordt de stadsbus gratis.`
- `Daarom sluiten wij een aantal straten af.`
- `Daarom geven we een groot feest voor hem.`

### dus = so / therefore

Examples:

- `Je hoeft zelf dus geen eten mee te nemen.`
- `De bus komt dus niet bij de halte.`
- `Er moeten dus altijd twee collega’s aan het werk zijn.`

### namelijk = because / the reason is that

`namelijk` explains the previous sentence.

Examples:

- `Op 1 mei wordt hij namelijk 67 jaar.`
- `Er zijn namelijk een paar feestdagen.`
- `Hij gaat namelijk verhuizen.`

## 4. Condition: als... dan...

### als = if / when

After `als`, the verb often moves to the end of the clause.

Examples:

- `Als jullie vragen hebben, hoor ik het graag.`
- `Als je de toets niet haalt, kun je een herkansing doen.`
- `Als u geen vlees eet, wilt u dit dan doorgeven?`
- `Als er een lift is, bezorgen wij op de vijfde verdieping.`

Exam trap: the condition changes the answer. For example, delivery to the fifth floor only happens if there is an elevator.

## 5. Change: what changed and what stayed the same

### veranderen = to change

Examples:

- `als jullie werktijden veranderen`
- `wat er verandert`

### verplaatsen / verplaatst naar = to move / reschedule to

Example:

- `Uw afspraak is verplaatst naar 16 oktober.`

### hetzelfde blijven = to stay the same

Examples:

- `De tijden blijven hetzelfde.`
- `Het tijdstip blijft hetzelfde.`

Classic exam trap:

| Text | Changes | Stays the same |
|---|---|---|
| Bus with temporary route | route / stop | times |
| Rescheduled appointment | date / dentist | time |

## 6. Dates, times, and frequency

### half + number

In Dutch, `half 11` means 10:30, not 11:30.

| Dutch | Actual time |
|---|---|
| half 9 | 08:30 or 20:30 depending on context |
| half 10 | 09:30 |
| half 11 | 10:30 |
| half 12 | 11:30 |
| half 7 | 18:30 |
| half 8 | 19:30 |
| half 9 in the evening | 20:30 |

### elke / iedere = every

- `elke dag` = every day
- `iedere week` = every week
- `elke eerste zondag` = every first Sunday
- `elke derde zondag` = every third Sunday

### per maand / per persoon / per weekend

- `per maand` = per month
- `per persoon` = per person
- `per weekend` = per weekend

### Frequencies

- `elke tien minuten` = every 10 minutes
- `elke twintig minuten` = every 20 minutes
- `elk kwartier` = every 15 minutes
- `elke dertig minuten` = every 30 minutes

## 7. Maximum, minimum, and at least

### maximaal = maximum / at most

Examples:

- `maximaal €15 per persoon`
- `maximaal twee weken vakantie`
- `maximaal één keer herkansing`

### minimaal = minimum / at least

Example:

- `minimaal op twee doordeweekse dagen`

### ten minste = at least

Example:

- `De levertijd is ten minste twee weken.`

Exam trap: `ten minste twee weken` is not exactly two weeks. It may be more.

## 8. Formality: u vs je/jij/jullie

### u

Used in formal texts: dentist, GP, school, services, public letters.

Examples:

- `U kunt bellen...`
- `Wilt u zich inschrijven?`
- `U hoeft geen medische gegevens te geven.`

### je / jij

Used in informal emails, colleagues, friends, class groups.

Examples:

- `Wil je mee met de bus?`
- `Laat je me weten wanneer jij kunt?`

### jullie

Informal plural: you all.

Examples:

- `Als jullie vragen hebben...`
- `Laten jullie mij weten...`

## 9. Simple passive voice with worden

A2 notices sometimes use passive voice.

Examples:

- `De kleedkamers worden verbouwd.` = The changing rooms are being renovated.
- `Auto's worden weggesleept.` = Cars are being towed away.

General form:

`worden + past participle`

You do not need deep grammatical analysis. Just recognize that the subject receives the action.

## 10. Most common traps in official simulations

### 1. Event date is different from deadline

Pattern examples:

- Party on April 24, but reply before April 15.
- Activity on September 16, but submit idea before August 1.
- Meeting on November 24, but notify before November 23.

### 2. Service opening hours are different from calling hours

Pattern examples:

- Dental practice open 8:30–17:00, but reception only answers during specific hours.
- GP open at one time, but test results only by phone at another time.

### 3. Something is possible, but only with a condition

Examples:

- Delivery to the fifth floor only if there is an elevator.
- Activity may cost 20 euros only if food is included.
- You may work two shifts in a row, but not after a night shift.

### 4. Required vs optional

Examples:

- Bring the theory book: required.
- Bring your own tools: not necessary.
- Shop experience: advantage, not required.
- Liking clothes: required.

### 5. Correct person for each task

Pattern examples:

- One person receives money.
- Another organizes the bus.
- Another has the gift list.
- One person receives texts for the book.
- Another receives registrations.

### 6. What is in the bag vs what the person has with them

A2 questions often ask which item is NOT in the object.

### 7. Old vs new

Examples:

- Old customer card can no longer be used; new card is free.
- Old appointment date changed; time stayed the same.
- Old route is not used; temporary stop is used.

### 8. Main event vs optional part

Examples:

- Sports day and barbecue have different prices/deadlines.
- People who do not join the sports day can still attend only the barbecue.

## 11. Reading strategy for the exam

For every text, follow this order:

1. Identify the text type: email, notice, table, invitation, service, rule.
2. Mark dates and times.
3. Mark obligation words: `moet`, `mag`, `hoeft`, `kan`, `maximaal`, `uiterlijk`.
4. Mark changes: `vanaf`, `verplaatst`, `tijdelijk`, `andere route`, `blijft hetzelfde`.
5. Find the responsible person: who receives the registration, money, phone call, or text.
6. Read the question before rereading the text.
7. Do not choose an answer only because you saw the same word. Check whether the condition is correct.

## 12. Model sentences to memorize

- `Laat mij weten of je kunt komen.` = Let me know if you can come.
- `U moet zich uiterlijk een maand van tevoren inschrijven.` = You must register no later than one month in advance.
- `De tijden blijven hetzelfde.` = The times stay the same.
- `Het tijdstip blijft hetzelfde.` = The appointment time stays the same.
- `U hoeft geen medische gegevens te geven.` = You do not have to provide medical data.
- `U moet wel zeggen hoe lang u denkt ziek te zijn.` = You do have to say how long you think you will be ill.
- `Als u vragen hebt, hoor ik het graag.` = If you have questions, I would be glad to hear them.
- `Neem contact met ons op.` = Contact us.
- `U kunt zich aanmelden via de website.` = You can register through the website.
- `De cursus bestaat uit zes lessen.` = The course consists of six lessons.
- `De winkel is vanaf nu elke eerste en derde zondag open.` = From now on, the shop is open every first and third Sunday of the month.
