# A2 Reading Simulation — Exam-Style Practice

> **Status:** original practice material inspired by the A2 reading exam style.
>
> **Important:** this is **not an official DUO exam** and it does **not copy official texts**. The texts and questions are original, but they follow common A2 reading patterns: short everyday texts, practical contexts, and multiple-choice questions.

## Reference points

- Official DUO/Inburgeren practice page: https://www.inburgeren.nl/examen-doen/oefenen.jsp
- DUO/Inburgeren English practice page: https://www.inburgeren.nl/en/taking-the-integration-exam/practicing.jsp
- Public exam-preparation information: A2 reading practice generally uses multiple-choice questions, everyday Dutch texts, and practical tasks such as reading letters, ads, schedules, notices, and short messages.

## How to use this simulation

Treat it like an exam:

1. Read the question first.
2. Look for the key information in the text.
3. Do not translate every word.
4. Pay attention to numbers, times, dates, negation, and exception words.
5. Check the answer key only at the end.

Suggested time: **65 minutes**.

Score guide:

- **0–14 correct:** review basic vocabulary and practice slow reading.
- **15–18 correct:** good start, but speed and precision still need work.
- **19–22 correct:** good A2 direction.
- **23–25 correct:** strong performance for this simulation.

# A2 reading strategy

## 1. Do not read like literature

In the exam, you do not need to appreciate the text. You need to find the answer.

Bad strategy:

> I will translate everything from beginning to end.

Better strategy:

> I will read the question, find the keyword, and confirm the sentence in the text.

## 2. Keywords to hunt for

- times: `om 8.00 uur`, `van 12.30 tot 13.00 uur`
- dates: `op maandag`, `vanaf 4 september`
- places: `in kamer 3`, `op school`, `bij de receptie`
- obligation: `moet`, `moeten`, `neem mee`
- prohibition: `niet toegestaan`, `mag niet`
- exception: `alleen`, `behalve`, `maar`
- duration: `drie weken`, `tot 16.00 uur`
- cost: `gratis`, `€ 15`, `per maand`

## 3. Beware of almost-correct answers

The exam often includes answers that use words from the text but change one detail.

Example:

Text:

> De bus vertrekt om 8.30 uur.

Wrong answer:

> De kinderen gaan met de trein.

The idea of transport appears, but the text says **bus**, not **train**.

## 4. How to review mistakes

For every mistake, ask:

1. Did I miss a word?
2. Did I read too fast?
3. Did I confuse a time/number/date?
4. Was the answer a trap?
5. Did I translate instead of scanning?
