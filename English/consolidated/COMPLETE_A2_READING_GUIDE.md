# Complete Guide — A2 Inburgering Reading

This is the central review file for **Lezen A2**. It consolidates vocabulary, grammar, and exam strategy with a focus on reading.

Goal: to read short everyday Dutch texts and answer multiple-choice questions in the A2 exam style.

---

# 1. How to think about the A2 reading exam

In the reading exam, you usually see:

- a question;
- a short text;
- 3 or 4 answer options;
- one correct answer.

The exam does not require you to translate everything. It checks whether you understand practical information:

- time;
- date;
- place;
- price;
- rule;
- reason;
- advice;
- obligation;
- prohibition;
- exception.

## Main strategy

1. Read the question first.
2. Mentally mark the keyword.
3. Read the text while looking for that information.
4. Confirm details: time, date, number, negation, condition.
5. Choose the option that answers the question exactly.

## The classic mistake

The most common mistake is choosing an answer option that contains a word from the text but does not answer the question.

Example:

Text:

> De bus vertrekt om 8.30 uur.

Question:

> Hoe gaan de kinderen naar het museum?

Wrong answer:

> Met de trein.

The topic of transport is present, but the text says **bus**, not **train**.

# 3. Core grammar for A2 reading

## 3.1 Basic word order

Main clause:

> subject + verb + rest

- Ik woon in Nederland.
- De les begint om 9.00 uur.
- De trein vertrekt van spoor 5.

## 3.2 Inversion

When the sentence starts with a time/place element, the verb usually comes immediately after it.

- Vandaag heb ik les.
- Morgen gaat hij werken.
- In de middag komt er regen.
- Vanaf september start de cursus.

For reading, this means: do not panic when the subject comes after the verb.

## 3.3 Questions

Yes/no question:

- Kom je morgen?
- Heeft u een afspraak?
- Is de winkel open?

Question with question word:

- Waar woont u?
- Wanneer begint de cursus?
- Hoeveel kost het?
- Waarom krijgt u deze brief?

## 3.4 Modal verbs

Modal verbs change the meaning of the sentence and usually push the other verb toward the end.

| Dutch | English |
|---|---|
| moeten | must / have to |
| kunnen | can / be able to |
| willen | want to |
| mogen | may / be allowed to |

Examples:

- U moet uw legitimatiebewijs meenemen.
- U kunt online betalen.
- Wij willen u informeren.
- U mag hier niet parkeren.

## 3.5 `want` vs `omdat`

Both mean “because”.

`want` keeps normal word order:

- Ik blijf thuis, want ik ben ziek.

`omdat` sends the verb to the end:

- Ik blijf thuis, omdat ik ziek ben.

For reading, recognize that both introduce a reason.

## 3.6 `daarom`

`daarom` = therefore / that is why.

- Het lokaal is niet beschikbaar. Daarom is de les online.
- De verwarming is kapot. Daarom werkt iedereen thuis.

## 3.7 `als`

`als` = if / when.

- Als u niet kunt komen, bel dan de praktijk.
- Als het regent, neem een paraplu mee.

## 3.8 `worden / wordt`

`worden` indicates change, future state, or passive voice.

- Het wordt koud. — It is getting cold / it will become cold.
- Mijn dochter wordt zeven. — My daughter is turning seven.
- Het huis wordt verkocht. — The house is being sold.
- De verwarming wordt gerepareerd. — The heating is being repaired.

Contrast:

- Het is koud. — It is cold now.
- Het wordt koud. — It is getting cold / will become cold.

## 3.9 Negation: `niet` vs `geen`

`geen` = no / not any / none.

- Ik heb geen auto.
- Er is geen lift.
- Ik heb geen tijd.

`niet` = not, used to negate verbs, adjectives, and sentence parts.

- Ik kom niet.
- De woning is niet geschikt.
- Water is niet inbegrepen.
- Huisdieren zijn niet toegestaan.

## 3.10 Comparison

| Dutch | English |
|---|---|
| meer | more |
| minder | less / fewer |
| groter | bigger |
| kleiner | smaller |
| beter | better |
| slechter | worse |
| dan | than |
| even ... als | as ... as |

Examples:

- De huur is hoger dan vorig jaar.
- Deze cursus is korter dan de andere cursus.

# 7. Checklist before answering

Before choosing an answer, ask:

1. Does the question ask **when**, **where**, **who**, **what**, or **why**?
2. Does the answer have the same time/date as the text?
3. Does the option include `niet`, `geen`, `alleen`, or `behalve`?
4. Is the option about an obligation or an option?
5. Does the text refer to `vroeger` or `tegenwoordig`?
6. Is the option true but not answering the question?
7. Is the word in the text similar but not the same? Example: bus/train, huisarts/apotheek, huur/koop.

# 8. Reading revision plan

## 20-minute routine

1. 5 min — review 20 high-frequency words.
2. 5 min — read one short text without a dictionary.
3. 5 min — answer questions.
4. 5 min — review mistakes and write down traps.

## 45-minute routine

1. 10 min — vocabulary by theme.
2. 20 min — short reading simulation.
3. 10 min — correction.
4. 5 min — write 5 sentences with new words.

## Errors to track

| Error type | Example |
|---|---|
| time/date | confused 8.15 with 8.30 |
| negation | ignored `niet toegestaan` |
| cost detail | ignored `niet inbegrepen` |
| purpose of text | did not understand why the letter was sent |
| correct advice | chose the wrong tip |
| vocabulary | did not know `ophalen`, `aanmelden`, `vertraging` |
| almost-correct option | similar word, different meaning |
