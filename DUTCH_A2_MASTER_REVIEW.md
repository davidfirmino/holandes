# Dutch A2 Master Review — Central Study Notes

A central study file for revising the most important Dutch seen so far, with a focus on **high-frequency words, core grammar, everyday Dutch, and Inburgering A2 reading/listening patterns**.

This file is written for a **Brazilian Portuguese speaker**, with occasional **English analogies** when useful.

---

## How to use this file

Use this file in 3 ways:

1. **Fast review**: read the sections “Core survival Dutch” and “Grammar that matters most”.
2. **Lesson review**: jump to `Les 1`, `Les 2`, `Les 9`, `Les 10`, `Les 11`.
3. **Active recall**: cover the Portuguese meanings and try to remember the Dutch, then reverse.

Recommended study rhythm:

- 10 min: read vocabulary aloud
- 10 min: translate sample sentences mentally
- 10 min: answer questions or write 5 sentences about your life

# 1. Core survival Dutch

These are the words that appear **all the time** in the exam and in real life.

## 1.1 Question words (`w-woorden`)

| Dutch | Portuguese | English hint | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| wat | o que | what | What? |
| waar | onde | where | Place |
| wie | quem | who | Person |
| wanneer | quando | when | Time |
| waarom | por que | why | Reason |
| hoe | como | how | Manner |
| welk / welke | qual / quais | which | `welk` with many `het` words; `welke` with `de` words and plural |
| hoeveel | quanto(s) | how much/many | Quantity |
| hoe laat | que horas | what time | Clock time |
| hoelang | quanto tempo | how long | Duration |

### Examples

- **Wat** doe je? → O que você faz?
- **Waar** woon je? → Onde você mora?
- **Wanneer** begint de les? → Quando a aula começa?
- **Waarom** leer je Nederlands? → Por que você aprende holandês?
- **Hoe** gaat het? → Como vai?
- **Welke** bus neem je? → Qual ônibus você pega?
- **Hoeveel** kost dat? → Quanto custa isso?

## 1.3 Connectors that build meaning

| Dutch | Portuguese | Main job |
|---|---|---|
| en | e | Add information |
| maar | mas | Contrast |
| of | ou | Choice |
| want | porque | Reason, normal word order |
| omdat | porque | Reason, verb goes to the end |
| dus | então / por isso | Result |
| daarom | por isso | Explicit reason/result |
| als | se / quando | Condition or time |
| dan | então / depois / do que | Sequence or comparison |
| eerst | primeiro | Sequence |
| daarna / dan | depois | Sequence |

### The crucial pair: `want` vs `omdat`

#### `want`
Keeps **normal order**.

- Ik blijf thuis, **want** ik ben moe.
- Eu fico em casa, porque estou cansado.

#### `omdat`
Pushes the verb to the **end**.

- Ik blijf thuis, **omdat** ik moe **ben**.
- Eu fico em casa, porque estou cansado.

### Memory trick

- `want` = easy “because”, normal order
- `omdat` = “because” + **verb at the end**

## 1.4 Time words

| Dutch | Portuguese | Note |
|---|---|---|
| nu | agora | now |
| vandaag | hoje | today |
| morgen | amanhã | tomorrow |
| gisteren | ontem | yesterday |
| straks | daqui a pouco | later soon |
| vroeger | antigamente / antes | vague past |
| tegenwoordig | hoje em dia | nowadays |
| geleden | atrás / há X tempo | always with time expression |
| meteen | imediatamente | immediately |
| eerst | primeiro | first |
| later | mais tarde | later |

### Key distinction: `vroeger` vs `geleden`

#### `vroeger`
Vague past, like “before / in the old days”.

- **Vroeger** woonde ik in Brazilië.
- Antigamente eu morava no Brasil.

#### `geleden`
Specific past distance, like “ago”.

- Twee jaar **geleden** verhuisde ik.
- Eu me mudei há dois anos.

# 2. Grammar that matters most

This section focuses on the grammar patterns that give the biggest return.

## 2.1 Normal sentence order in main clauses

Very often:

**Subject + verb + rest**

- Ik woon in Nederland.
- Wij drinken koffie.
- De docent geeft les.

---

## 2.2 Inversion after time or place at the beginning

If you start with a time word, the verb usually comes before the subject.

- **Vandaag heb** ik les.
- **Morgen ga** ik werken.
- **In de winter regent** het vaak.

### Portuguese comparison
In Portuguese you can often keep the subject after a time expression naturally:
- Hoje eu trabalho.

In Dutch, this pattern is very common:
- Vandaag **werk** ik.

---

## 2.3 Questions

### Yes/no question
Verb first.

- Woon je in Nederland?
- Heb je kinderen?
- Ga je morgen werken?

### Information question
Question word + verb + subject.

- Waar woon je?
- Wanneer begint de les?
- Waarom ben je moe?

---

## 2.4 `gaan` = “go” and “going to”

### Literal movement
- Ik ga naar school. → Vou para a escola.

### Near future / intention
- Ik ga koken. → Vou cozinhar.
- Wij gaan studeren. → Vamos estudar.

### English analogy
`gaan + infinitive` often works like **be going to**.

---

## 2.5 Modal verbs + infinitive

The infinitive usually stays near the end.

- Ik **moet** morgen **werken**.
- Ik **kan** je **helpen**.
- Wij **willen** een huis **kopen**.
- Je **mag** hier niet **roken**.

---

## 2.6 `want` vs `omdat`

Already seen above, but this is worth repeating because it is central.

### `want`
- Ik blijf thuis, want ik ben moe.

### `omdat`
- Ik blijf thuis, omdat ik moe ben.

### More examples
- Ik leer Nederlands, want ik woon in Nederland.
- Ik leer Nederlands, omdat ik in Nederland woon.

---

## 2.7 `lusten` vs `houden van`

This is one of the most important meaning differences for daily life.

### `lusten`
Used mostly for **food and drink**.

- Ik lust koffie. → Eu gosto de café.
- Ik lust geen vis. → Eu não gosto de peixe.

### `houden van`
Used for general liking, affection, or love.

- Ik hou van mijn familie. → Eu amo / gosto muito da minha família.
- Ik hou van muziek. → Eu gosto de música.

### Quick rule
- food/drink → usually `lusten`
- general liking/love → usually `houden van`

---

## 2.8 Negation: `niet` vs `geen`

This has not been the central focus yet, but it is **extremely important** and appears everywhere.

### `geen`
Used for:
- no / not a / not any with nouns without a definite article
- zero quantity

Examples:
- Ik heb **geen** auto. → Eu não tenho carro.
- Er is **geen** wifi in het huis. → Não há wifi na casa.
- Ik drink **geen** melk. → Eu não bebo leite.

### `niet`
Used for:
- verbs
- adjectives
- adverbs
- specific things
- the rest of the sentence

Examples:
- Ik werk **niet** vandaag. → Eu não trabalho hoje.
- Het huis is **niet** groot. → A casa não é grande.
- Ik kom **niet** morgen. → Eu não venho amanhã.

### Very simple memory rule
- `geen` = no / none
- `niet` = not

---

## 2.9 Articles: `de` and `het`

Dutch has two main definite articles.

- **de**
- **het**

### Important reality
You often just need to **memorize the article with the noun**.

Examples:
- **de** school
- **het** huis
- **de** keuken
- **het** weer

### Helpful rough rules (not perfect)
- plural nouns usually take **de**
- many common-person nouns take **de**
- diminutives (`-je`) take **het**

Examples:
- de kinderen
- de docent
- het huisje
- het koekje

---

## 2.10 Impersonal weather with `het`

In weather expressions, Dutch often uses **het**, similar to English **it**.

- **Het** regent. → Está chovendo.
- **Het** waait. → Está ventando.
- **Het** vriest. → Está congelando / geando.

### English analogy
- It rains → Het regent
- It is cold → Het is koud

# 9. Most important contrast pairs seen so far

## `want` vs `omdat`
- want = because, normal order
- omdat = because, verb final

## `vroeger` vs `geleden`
- vroeger = before / in the old days
- geleden = ago

## `lusten` vs `houden van`
- lusten = food/drink preference
- houden van = general liking/love

## `doen` vs `maken`
- doen = do, general action
- maken = make, produce, create

## `het weer` vs `weer`
- het weer = the weather
- weer = again

## `de kou` vs `koud`
- de kou = the cold (noun)
- koud = cold (adjective)

## `welk` vs `welke`
- welk = often with `het` singular nouns
- welke = often with `de` nouns and plurals

## `boven` vs `beneden`
- boven = upstairs / above
- beneden = downstairs / below

## `naast` vs `tegenover`
- naast = next to
- tegenover = opposite / across from

# 11. Most important exam-reading habits

These habits help a lot in Inburgering A2.

## 11.1 Look first for:

- numbers
- times
- dates
- places
- who is doing what
- whether something is allowed or not
- frequency words (`vaak`, `soms`, `nooit`)
- contrast words (`maar`, `want`, `omdat`, `dus`)

## 11.2 In a reading question, ask yourself:

1. What is the topic? (house, school, food, weather?)
2. Is the question about:
   - place?
   - time?
   - quantity?
   - preference?
   - permission?
3. Which exact line answers it?

## 11.3 Common traps in A2 reading

- answer choice changes one small detail
- text says “soms”, answer says “altijd”
- text says “vijf minuten lopen”, answer says “met de trein”
- text says “geen”, answer option removes the negation

# 15. Final teacher notes

## What matters most right now

For your current stage, the biggest wins are:

1. recognizing high-frequency words quickly
2. understanding small grammar words
3. reading short texts without translating every word
4. building confidence with simple correct Dutch

## What not to do

- do not try to memorize everything at once
- do not study only isolated vocabulary without sentences
- do not ignore small words like `nog`, `al`, `want`, `omdat`, `dan`

## What to do instead

- read simple sentences aloud
- revisit the same words several times
- write short personal sentences
- compare Dutch with Portuguese and English when helpful

## Most important mindset

At A2 level, you do **not** need “beautiful Dutch”.
You need:

- useful Dutch
- recognizable Dutch
- understandable Dutch
- exam-functional Dutch

That is enough to make real progress.
