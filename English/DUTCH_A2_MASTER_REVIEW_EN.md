# Dutch A2 Master Review — English Version

A central study file for reviewing the most important Dutch seen so far, with a focus on **high-frequency words, core grammar, everyday Dutch, and Inburgering A2 reading/listening patterns**.

This version is written entirely in English, except for Dutch vocabulary and Dutch example sentences.

---

## How to use this file

Use this file in three ways:

1. **Fast review:** read “Core survival Dutch” and “Grammar that matters most”.
2. **Lesson review:** jump to the theme you want to revise.
3. **Active recall:** cover the English meaning and try to remember the Dutch; then reverse.

Recommended study rhythm:

- 10 minutes: read vocabulary aloud.
- 10 minutes: translate sample sentences mentally.
- 10 minutes: answer questions or write five sentences about your life.

# 1. Core survival Dutch

These are the words that appear constantly in the exam and in real life.

## 1.1 Question words

| Dutch | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| wat | what | asks about a thing/action |
| waar | where | asks about place |
| wie | who | asks about a person |
| wanneer | when | asks about time/date |
| waarom | why | asks about reason |
| hoe | how | asks about manner/condition |
| welk / welke | which | `welk` often with singular `het` words; `welke` with `de` words and plurals |

| hoe laat | what time | clock time |
| hoelang | how long | duration |
| hoe duur | how expensive / how much does it cost | price |
| waarnaartoe | where to | destination |

Examples:

- **Wat** doe je? — What are you doing?
- **Waar** woon je? — Where do you live?
- **Wanneer** begint de les? — When does the lesson start?
- **Waarom** leer je Nederlands? — Why are you learning Dutch?
- **Hoe** gaat het? — How are you?
- **Welke** bus neem je? — Which bus do you take?
- **Hoeveel** kost dat? — How much does that cost?

## 1.3 Connectors

| Dutch | English | Main function |
|---|---|---|
| en | and | addition |
| maar | but | contrast |
| of | or | choice |
| want | because / for | reason, normal word order |
| omdat | because | reason, verb goes to the end |
| dus | so / therefore | result |
| daarom | therefore / that is why | explicit result |
| als | if / when | condition or time |
| dan | then / than | sequence or comparison |
| eerst | first | sequence |
| daarna | after that | sequence |

### `want` vs `omdat`

Both can mean **because**, but the grammar changes.

`want` keeps normal word order:

- Ik blijf thuis, **want** ik ben moe.  
  I stay home because I am tired.

`omdat` pushes the verb to the end:

- Ik blijf thuis, **omdat** ik moe **ben**.  
  I stay home because I am tired.

More examples:

- Ik leer Nederlands, **want** ik woon in Nederland.
- Ik leer Nederlands, **omdat** ik in Nederland **woon**.

Memory trick:

- `want` = easy because, normal order.
- `omdat` = because + verb at the end.

## 1.4 Time words

| Dutch | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| nu | now | current moment |
| vandaag | today | current day |
| morgen | tomorrow | next day |
| gisteren | yesterday | previous day |
| straks | later soon / in a bit | very common in daily Dutch |
| vroeger | formerly / in the past | vague past |
| tegenwoordig | nowadays | present era |
| geleden | ago | used with a time expression |
| meteen | immediately | right away |

### `vroeger` vs `geleden`

`vroeger` = vague past, like “before / in the old days”.

- **Vroeger** woonde ik in Brazilië.  
  I used to live in Brazil.

`geleden` = specific distance in the past, like “ago”.

- Twee jaar **geleden** verhuisde ik.  
  I moved two years ago.

# 2. Grammar that matters most

## 2.1 Basic main clause order

Very often:

**Subject + verb + rest**

- Ik woon in Nederland.
- Wij drinken koffie.
- De docent geeft les.

---

## 2.2 Inversion after time/place at the beginning

If a time or place element starts the sentence, the verb usually comes before the subject.

- **Vandaag heb** ik les. — Today I have class.
- **Morgen ga** ik werken. — Tomorrow I am going to work.
- **In de winter regent** het vaak. — In winter it often rains.

---

## 2.3 Questions

Yes/no question: verb first.

- Woon je in Nederland?
- Heb je kinderen?
- Ga je morgen werken?

Information question: question word + verb + subject.

- Waar woon je?
- Wanneer begint de les?
- Waarom ben je moe?

---

## 2.4 `gaan` = go and going to

Literal movement:

- Ik ga naar school. — I go to school.

Near future / intention:

- Ik ga koken. — I am going to cook.
- Wij gaan studeren. — We are going to study.

English analogy: `gaan + infinitive` often works like **be going to**.

---

## 2.5 Modal verbs + infinitive

The infinitive usually appears near the end.

- Ik **moet** morgen **werken**. — I have to work tomorrow.
- Ik **kan** je **helpen**. — I can help you.
- Wij **willen** een huis **kopen**. — We want to buy a house.
- Je **mag** hier niet **roken**. — You are not allowed to smoke here.

---

## 2.6 `worden` and `wordt`

`worden` means **to become / to get / to be made into**.

Most important forms:

| Subject | Form |
|---|---|
| ik | word |
| jij / hij / zij / het / u | wordt |
| wij / jullie / zij | worden |

Main meanings:

### Change of state

- Het **wordt** koud. — It is getting cold / it will become cold.
- Het **wordt** donker. — It is getting dark.
- Morgen **wordt** het zonnig. — Tomorrow it will become sunny.

### Profession / becoming something

- Hij **wordt** dokter. — He is becoming / will become a doctor.
- Zij **wordt** docent. — She is becoming / will become a teacher.

### Age

- Mijn dochter **wordt** zeven jaar. — My daughter is turning seven.

### Passive voice

- Het huis **wordt** verkocht. — The house is being sold.
- Hier **wordt** niet gerookt. — Smoking is not done here / no smoking here.

Memory contrast:

- Het **is** koud. — It is cold now.
- Het **wordt** koud. — It is getting cold / will be cold.

---

## 2.7 `lusten` vs `houden van`

`lusten` is mostly for food and drink.

- Ik lust koffie. — I like coffee.
- Ik lust geen vis. — I do not like fish.

`houden van` is for general liking, affection, or love.

- Ik hou van mijn familie. — I love my family.
- Ik hou van muziek. — I like music.

Quick rule:

- food/drink → often `lusten`
- general liking/love → often `houden van`

---

## 2.8 `niet` vs `geen`

`geen` is used for “no / not a / not any” with nouns.

- Ik heb **geen** auto. — I do not have a car.
- Er is **geen** wifi in het huis. — There is no Wi-Fi in the house.
- Ik drink **geen** melk. — I do not drink milk.

`niet` is used for verbs, adjectives, adverbs, specific things, and many other sentence parts.

- Ik werk **niet** vandaag. — I am not working today.
- Het huis is **niet** groot. — The house is not big.
- Ik kom **niet** morgen. — I am not coming tomorrow.

Simple memory rule:

- `geen` = no / none / not any
- `niet` = not

---

## 2.9 `de` and `het`

Dutch has two main definite articles:

- **de**
- **het**

You usually need to memorize the article with the noun.

Examples:

- **de** school
- **het** huis
- **de** keuken
- **het** weer

Useful rough rules:

- plural nouns usually take **de**
- many people nouns take **de**
- diminutives ending in `-je` take **het**

Examples:

- de kinderen
- de docent
- het huisje
- het koekje

---

## 2.10 Impersonal weather with `het`

In weather expressions, Dutch often uses **het**, similar to English **it**.

- **Het** regent. — It rains / it is raining.
- **Het** waait. — It is windy / it is blowing.
- **Het** vriest. — It is freezing.
- **Het** dooit. — It is thawing.

# 4. A2 reading strategy

The A2 reading exam is not about translating every word. It is about finding the exact information needed.

## 4.1 Best strategy

1. Read the question first.
2. Underline or mentally mark the keyword.
3. Scan the text for the keyword or related words.
4. Check numbers, times, dates, negation, and exception words.
5. Choose the best answer.

## 4.2 Common traps

| Trap | Example |
|---|---|
| similar transport words | bus vs trein vs tram |
| similar times | 8.15 vs 8.30 |
| negation | toegestaan vs niet toegestaan |
| partial truth | one answer is true but does not answer the question |
| old vs current situation | vroeger vs tegenwoordig |
| price details | inclusief vs niet inbegrepen |

## 4.3 Words to hunt for in exam texts

- `om 8.00 uur` — at 8:00
- `van 12.30 tot 13.00 uur` — from 12:30 to 13:00
- `op maandag` — on Monday
- `vanaf 4 september` — from September 4
- `moet / moeten` — must / have to
- `niet toegestaan` — not allowed
- `mag niet` — may not / not allowed
- `alleen` — only
- `behalve` — except
- `gratis` — free
- `gesloten` — closed
- `inbegrepen` — included
- `vertraging` — delay
- `afwezig` — absent
